Regression Lines in Regular Polygons: A Statistical Curiosity

March 15, 2024 By Diganta Bhattacharya

One hurdle for every B.Stat student to cross in the final year is the Statistics comprehensive viva where every one of us had to face questions from every stat course professor present in the interview panel. One way to prepare ourselves naturally was creating problems in statistics and solving them in groups. Here is one intriguing problem that Souvik and I came up with in those discussions:

The Problem

Let's say there is an equilateral triangle on the XY plane. Taking the three vertices as data points, a regression line can be drawn on the plane. If we rotate this triangle, keeping the centroid fixed, how does the regression line change?

The Insight

The quickest insight to this problem was proposed by Sayak Chatterjee. Evaluating the slope of the line directly for various rotations, he showed that in all of the cases the regression line was parallel to the X-axis! With that information in mind, it's easy to guess that the line had to be the constant line passing through the centroid.

Think about the implication of this result: there is no LINEAR dependence between the X and Y coordinates.
