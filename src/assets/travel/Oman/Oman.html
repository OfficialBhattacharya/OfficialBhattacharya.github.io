Highlights & Attractions

Jebel Shams

The highest peak in Oman, known as the "Mountain of the Sun." Offers breathtaking views and is part of the spectacular Hajar Mountains range.

Wadi Shab

A stunning oasis with turquoise pools and dramatic canyon walls. Perfect for swimming and exploring hidden caves behind waterfalls.

Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque

One of the largest mosques in the world, featuring exquisite Islamic architecture, beautiful gardens, and the world's second-largest hand-woven carpet.

Muttrah Souq

One of the oldest markets in the Arab world, offering traditional Omani handicrafts, frankincense, silver jewelry, and spices.

Wahiba Sands

A spectacular desert with golden sand dunes reaching up to 100 meters high. Experience Bedouin culture and stunning desert landscapes.

Nizwa Fort

A 17th-century fortress with a massive circular tower. Explore the historic capital of Oman and its traditional architecture.

Coastal Beauty

Over 3,000 km of pristine coastline with dramatic cliffs, secluded beaches, and rich marine life. Perfect for dolphin watching and diving.

Frankincense Trail

UNESCO World Heritage site showcasing the ancient frankincense trade routes. Explore Salalah and the Land of Frankincense.

Omani Culture & Heritage

Hospitality

Omanis are renowned for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature. Traditional coffee (qahwa) and dates are offered to guests as a sign of respect.

Traditional Arts

Rich tradition of handicrafts including silver jewelry, pottery, weaving, and woodcarving. Omani daggers (khanjar) are symbols of national pride.

Music & Dance

Traditional Omani music features drums, flutes, and string instruments. Folk dances like the Razha are performed during celebrations and festivals.

Architecture

Distinctive Islamic architecture with wind towers, courtyards, and intricate geometric patterns. Forts and castles reflect Oman's rich history.

Cuisine

Traditional Omani cuisine features rice, meat, fish, and aromatic spices. Shuwa (slow-cooked lamb) and Omani halwa are popular delicacies.

Maritime Heritage

Oman's seafaring tradition spans centuries, with dhow building being a cherished craft. The country was a major maritime trading power.
